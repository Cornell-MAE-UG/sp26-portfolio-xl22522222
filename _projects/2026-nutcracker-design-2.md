## Find
A design for a nutcracker that can crack a macademia nut without deforming more than 2% of length, as well as its location of maximum deflection

## Assume:
- Only consider transverse loads
- No thermal expansion
- No friction, external loads
- Handle of nutcracker can be approximated as a straight beam

## Given
- FN = 2237.68N (from previous calculation in part 1)

## Approach
- Use formula for maximum deflection found in Appendix E to find minimum I
- Select reasonable shape for cross section and material, calculate dimensions using I

## Discussion
My nutcracker was about 27m in length, which is slightly larger than the average nut cracker but was still fully usable and functional. It also met my requirements and is capable of supplying enough force to crack a macadamia nut of any reasonable size, between 1.5cm-3cm, which is the size of an average nut I got from the web. To achieve this, a grip strength of 40kg is required, which is fully possible for a very average adult. I found the location of maximum deflection of my nutcracker to be 15.3cm from the end of the handle, and my design has a maximum deflection of exactly 2% of the length. To achieve this, I used a hollow circular cross-section with thickness 1mm to ensure that the handle is easy to grip, while maintaining maximum mass-efficiency. I selected steel, a material with large young's modulus to ensure that it is a very ridgid material.
